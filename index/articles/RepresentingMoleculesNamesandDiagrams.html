Representing Molecules: Names and Diagrams

Introduction

Atoms are the building blocks of all matter. But that was just the beginning. In this article, we focus on how atoms combine into bigger units, molecules. We will also cover how we draw and name molecules.


Bonds

Essentially, atoms bond together by electrostatic attraction between their electrons and nuclei. This happens when atoms share or transfer electrons to achieve a stable electron configuration.

A stable electron configuration is one where the outermost shell (valence shell) is full. This typically means having 8 electrons in the valence shell.

However there are exceptions, which will be covered in another time.



Types of Bonds

There are three main types of bonds: ionic, covalent, and metallic.


-Ionic Bonds

By far the simplest of the three. This happens when electrons are transferred from one atom to another, creating charged ions that attract each other. Usually this happens between a metal and a non-metal.
